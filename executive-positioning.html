Why this is harder than it should be

Strong performance is necessary. It is not sufficient.

At the Director and VP level, everyone in the room is competent. What separates the ones who advance is how clearly they communicate strategic impact, organizational influence, and the kind of future value that C-suite decisions are actually made on.

Execution is assumed.

Decision-makers at the C-suite level are not evaluating your track record of getting things done. They are evaluating whether you think and communicate like someone who belongs at the table they're sitting at.

Generic advice makes you sound like everyone else.

Professionals are overwhelmed by personal branding content and AI-generated messaging that all hits the same notes. None of it is built around what makes you specifically worth promoting.

You don't have time to figure this out alone.

You are still running your current role at full intensity. The strategy work — the narrative, the positioning, the networking — keeps getting pushed to the weekend that never comes.

The gap is rarely about capability.

The professionals who get passed over are not less capable than the ones who don't. They are less visible, less legible to decision-makers, and less positioned for the role above them. That is fixable.
